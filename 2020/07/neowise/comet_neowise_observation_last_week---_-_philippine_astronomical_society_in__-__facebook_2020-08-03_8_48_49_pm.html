Comet NEOWISE Observation

Last week, the comet was visible before sunrise. It then hid behind the glare of the Sun for several days. Fortunately, starting today, Comet NEOWISE will be visible again in our sky right after sunset just over the NW horizon.

We will be doing an #AstroAtHome facebook live episode later at 7 pm about this. For now, here are some information about the comet.

Where should I look in the night sky?

For those who were not able to observe it last week, try to look for it right after sunset from July 15 onwards. The Sun will set somewhere between the W and NW horizon. Look to the right of the spot where the Sun set and you will see the famous asterism called Big Dipper which belongs to the constellation Ursa Major. Use the stars Dubhe and Merak of the Big Dipper to help you locate it. The comet will be just below these two stars several minutes after sunset and to the right Merak and Dubhe after a week. Check the photo below for the change in its location relative to the Big Dipper the next several days.

Comet NEOWISE, now located in the evening sky, will slowly rise to a higher position every night making it easier to spot if you happen to have obstruction of the sky.

It will be closest to Earth or in its perigee by July 23 at a distance of about 103 million km. It was in its perihelion or closest approach to the Sun last July 3, 2020.

Why is it called NEOWISE?

It was discovered not by a person, but by NASA's orbiting satellite telescope NEOWISE or Near Earth Object Wide-field Infrared Survey Explorer only last March 27, 2020 hence its name.

How can I have a better view of the comet?

Comet NEOWISE can be seen with the naked eye but a pair of binoculars or low magnification of telescopes will give you a better view. Later this month, it is expected to reach up to 30 degrees above the horizon over the constellation Coma Berenices.

If I missed viewing it, can I see the comet again next month?

If you missed the chance to view it this July, you won't see the comet ever again. It is now heading back towards the outer parts of the solar system. It truly is a once in a lifetime chance because Comet NEOWISE will return in almost 7000 years.

- Edmund Rosales, Astronomer

_______

We only have a limited number of nights to get a chance to view this celestial wonder. Let's hope for clear skies this month!

Stay safe and keep looking up!
